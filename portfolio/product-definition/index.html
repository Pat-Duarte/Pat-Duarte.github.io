Product definition

The Company

A former software factory looking to reinvent itself as a product company. They had built the same product several times for different clients and wanted to use that as the starting point for a product.

What they had

They had a set of microservices in place, several versions of custom digital wallets made for clients, and a web aplication where clients could see reports and access settings.

They had created this diagram with information on all the pieces of software they had.

The problem

Even if they had already developed all this software, they didnâ€™t have a clear idea of what the product was going to be. Sales people talked to clients following old practices of finding out what the client wanted and then communicating that to the development team. This needed to change. There had to be a proper definition of the product. On their end, developers were also lacking the big picture of what they were working towards.

First steps

The first goal was to turn the diagram they provided into something resembling a product. Working together with the people who knew the company and the software they already had we arrived at a grouping of the pieces from the original diagram that could work as the basis for the product definition. We rearranged the elements until we achieved that first goal. As a bonus, we defined names for the main components of the product.

Initial definition

Once all the stakeholders agreed on the components of the product and how they interacted with each other, we validated the names and created concise definitions for each of those main pillars.

At the same time, we worked to create the new image for the new product. We defined a new color palette, and a style for the visual assets we would use.

Visual support

Next, we wanted to incorporate the visuals to go with what we were saying with words.

Full definition and website

The final step was showing the world what the product was about so we set out to create a full definition of each component and how they worked together. The final version consisted of a new website that was clear enough for people to understand a complex product.
